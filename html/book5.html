Gone Girl

Summary

"Gone Girl" by Gillian Flynn is a psychological thriller centered around the disappearance of Amy Dunne and the subsequent media frenzy and police investigation. The story unfolds through the alternating perspectives of Amy and her husband, Nick Dunne. On their fifth wedding anniversary, Amy goes missing under suspicious circumstances, and Nick becomes the prime suspect in her disappearance. As the investigation progresses, it is revealed through Amy's diary entries that their marriage was troubled and filled with secrets. The story builds tension with unexpected twists and turns, making it a gripping and unsettling read.
