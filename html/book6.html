Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

Summary

"Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone" by J.K. Rowling introduces readers to Harry Potter, an orphaned boy who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard. Raised by his unkind aunt and uncle, Harry learns that he has been accepted to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. At Hogwarts, Harry makes friends with Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, and uncovers a magical world filled with wondrous creatures, spells, and mysteries. As he navigates his new life, he learns about his famous past and his connection to the dark wizard Voldemort, who killed his parents. The central plot revolves around Harry and his friends uncovering a plot to steal the Sorcerer's Stone, a magical object with the power to grant immortality. With bravery and cleverness, Harry confronts various challenges, including enchanted traps and magical creatures, to prevent the stone from falling into the wrong hands.
